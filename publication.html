Security & Prviacy

Privacy preserving crowd computing with encrypted processing and training

Status

Still undergoing audition by ICDCS2021.

Abstract

Crowd computing is a form of distributed computing where both the data and the computation are crowdsourced from many people (i.e. data owners) and many computing devices across the internet by commissioning entities. A crowd computing system usually involves a centralised crowd computing platform, which is assumed to be trusted by all parties. Existing crowd computing systems tend to focus on application protocols instead of addressing security and privacy issues. Platform companies or the commissioning entities may re-use or re-sell data without permission and knowledge due to oversight in operating procedures or dishonest intent. This paper presents a practical cryptographic solution with new protocol and architecture to tackle the above issues. Our protocol uses Homomorphic encryption efficiency for data protection and a blockchain for traceability of all activities. Our scheme enables asynchronous operations between all parties and without any trusted third parties. Our proof of concept is applied on logistic regression using credit data. The result shows that the generated predictive models' accuracy does not receive a significant accuracy penalty and is comparable to the predictive model trained using the full data set.
